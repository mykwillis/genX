Books on Evolutionary Computing

There are massive volumes of information available in the field of evolutionary computing in general, and genetic algorithms in particular.  For those interested in starting advanced study or research in the field, it can be daunting to figure out where to start.

The books listed below constitute our recommendations for the "must-read" books that should be first on your list.

John Holland's book is the fountainhead from which focused research on genetic algorithms sprang forth.  Adaptation in Natural and Artificial Systems is not the most accessible tome for a non-scientist, though, and as such is recommended to be tackled by those wishing to understand more rigorously the mechanisms by which genetic algorithms work.
David Goldberg, like many of the original pioneers in the study and applications of genetic algorithms, was a student of John Holland's at the University of Michigan.  His book, Genetic Algorithms in Search, Optimization, and Machine Learning, is a wonderful and accessible introduction to the field.
Melanie Mitchell's book introduces the reader to many areas of research in genetic algorithms without getting too bogged down in details to be unreadable by the non-scientist.  An Introduction to Genetic Algorithms serves best as a springboard to interesting areas of research that should be read after a thorough grounding has been gained from Goldberg.
