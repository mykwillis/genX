Selecting Individuals for Reproduction

Selection is the process by which individuals are chosen to be parents for individuals in the next generation.  Selection is normally done so that more highly-fit individuals are chosen as parents more often than less-fit individuals, and this is usually done in a manner that introduces some randomness.

There are quite a few possible ways to implement selection, and a rather substantial amount of research has been done toward determining which methods are best in various situations.  genX provides many of the most popular methods, and even allows custom selection methods to be supplied by you.  By default, genX uses something called Roulette selection, which gives individuals a propability of being selected that is proportionate to their relative fitness in the population.

You can experiment with the effects of different selection methods by setting the SelectionMethod property of the GA component.
