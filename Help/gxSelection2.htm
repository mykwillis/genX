Selection

Some terms that are thrown around in selection schemes:

stochastic - probabilistic.  In stochastic selection schemes, there is no guarantee made as to whether or not an individual will be allowed to reproduce, there are only probabilities assigned to that occurance.

sampling with replacement - After selecting an individual from the population, that individual is put back in 'the pool' of candidates for the next selection.  This means that the probability of an individual being selected is not affected by how many times it has already been chosen. 

sampling without replacement - After selecting an individual from the population, that individual is not put back in the pool.  Actually, it is put back in the pool, but its selection probability is reduced by 1.0.  This makes sure that any individual will never have more than f/ft + 1 instances in the next generation.

 

Deterministic.  This method selects individuals according to their expected selection count ( pselect * n ).  Each individual is automatically selected at the number of times given by the integer portion of e.  The population is then sorted according to the remainders, and the indiviuals with the highest remainders are selected until the desired number has been chosen.
