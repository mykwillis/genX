Glossary

[The glossary is still under construction.]

genetic algorithm
A methodology for evolving a population of candidate solutions to a problem using biologically-motivated operators such as mutation and recombination.
syntenic
Refers to genes present on the same chromosome. Non-syntenic genes are inherited independendently of one another, while syntenic genes may have some linkage.
heterozygous
Having different alleles at the corresponding gene loci on homologous chromosomes. In other words, a heterozygous individual has the potential to pass on either of multiple allele values for a given gene.
genotype
The sum of all genetic information posessed by an organism.
phenotype 
The physical expression of the genotype.  At a low level, proteins are the most direct product of genes, but the phenotype usually refers to a more directly observable trait.
genome
A complete set of haploid genes.  Thus, a diploid cell (such as a normal animal cell) would contain two genomes, since it contains two sets of genes. 
organism 
An individual living thing.
